What is Drone Mapping?

Drone mapping is when we use special flying robots (drones) to take hundreds of pictures of the ground from up in the sky. These pictures are then stitched together by computer software to create detailed maps and 3D models of large areas.

Instead of an archaeologist walking across a huge field for days, a drone can fly over it in just a few hours and capture everything from above! This is especially helpful because drones can see patterns and features that are hard to notice when you're walking on the ground.

üéØ Why Use Drones for Archaeology?

Drones can spot things that are invisible from the ground! Ancient buildings, buried roads, and old field patterns often show up as different colors or shapes in the soil and plants. From above, these patterns become clear and easy to see!

How Drone Mapping Works

1

Plan the Flight

We program the drone's flight path on a computer, telling it exactly where to fly and how high to go.

2

Take Off and Capture

The drone flies automatically over the area, taking hundreds or thousands of overlapping photos.

3

Process the Images

Special software stitches all the photos together to create detailed maps and 3D models.

4

Analyze the Results

Archaeologists (and our AI!) study the maps to find interesting spots to investigate further.
